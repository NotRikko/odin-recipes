Korean Army Stew

Description

Korean Army Stew otherwise known as Budae Jigae is a popular street and bar food that originates from Korea. With a popular and exquisite tangy taste, this is definitely a meal to enjoy on a night of drinking and excitement.

Ingredients

  • Gochugaru (Korean Red Chili Pepper Flakes)
  • Gochujang (Korean Red Chili Pepper Paste)
  • Soy Sauce
  • Fish Sauce
  • Mirin
  • Sugar
  • Black Pepper
  • Garlic
  • Chicken Stock

Stew Toppings (Optional)

  • Kimchi
  • Rice Cakes
  • Spam
  • Hotdog
  • Tofu
  • Green Onion
  • American Cheese
  • Instant Ramen Noodles

Steps

  1. Make the seasoning sauce. In a medium bowl, mix together the sauce ingredients (gochugaru, gochujang, soy sauce, fish sauce, rice wine, sugar, black pepper, minced garlic, and chicken stock) until combined. Set aside for now.
  2. Prepare the stew ingredients. Soak the frozen rice cakes in water for 15 minutes if frozen. Slice the spam, hot dog and other desired toppings into ¼ pieces. Chop the napa cabbage into bite-sized pieces. Wedge cut the onion. Diagnoally slice the green onion. Slice the stems off the mushrooms.
  3. Cook the ingredients. In a large shallow pot or braiser, add the kimchi, spam, hot dog and other chosen ingredients. Pour in the chicken stock and add the seasoning sauce in the center. Cover and cook on medium-high heat until the stock begins to boil.
  4. Add the ramen, rice cakes, green onion, and cheese. Add the ramen, rice cakes, green onion and cheese. Continue cooking uncovered for an additional 2-3 minutes or until the noodles soften.
  5. Optional. Serve with steamed rice and other korean side dishes.
